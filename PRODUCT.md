# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, engineering hiring managers, founders, and fellow builders who land on Mehul Dadlani's personal site. They arrive from a resume link, a GitHub profile, a LinkedIn DM, or a hackathon intro. Most scan for 20-40 seconds: they want to know quickly "what is this person exceptional at, and is the work real?" A smaller set (potential collaborators, crypto-native teams) read deeper into the project and hackathon story.

## Product Purpose

A personal-brand portfolio that makes Mehul look unmistakably impressive at a glance and rewards a deeper read. It exists to establish a single, memorable identity: a **product engineer who ships mobile end-to-end (Flutter), with a genuine Web3 and security spine** and a track record of real production ownership (100K+ install app, 50+ releases) plus hackathon wins. Success = a visitor leaving able to repeat that one-line positioning, with the evidence to believe it.

## Brand Personality

Confident, precise, and quietly cinematic. Voice is plain and concrete: numbers and shipped outcomes, not adjectives. Three words: **engineered, premium, distinctive**. It should feel like a focused instrument built by someone who sweats details, not a templated dev portfolio. Emotional goal: respect first, then intrigue.

## Anti-references

- Generic "dev portfolio template" sites (gradient hero, three identical project cards, skill progress bars).
- The Web3 cliche: purple/violet neon glow, holographic gradients, crypto-bro maximalism.
- The security cliche: matrix-green terminal rain, hacker-hoodie imagery.
- SaaS landing-page scaffolding: an uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section, big-gradient-number metric cards, "Trusted by" logo theatre.
- Anything that reads "AI generated default" on sight.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the receipts.** Lead with hard, verifiable numbers (100K+ users, 50+ releases, $2K+ won) over soft claims. Evidence is the design.
2. **One sharp signal.** A single amber accent and one type system carry the whole identity. Restraint reads as confidence.
3. **Own the intersection.** The mobile + web3 + security blend is the differentiator and must be unmissable, not buried in a skills list.
4. **Depth, not decoration.** Motion and layering exist to create a premium, cinematic feel and guide attention, never as filler.
5. **Dodge the category reflex.** If the aesthetic is guessable from "web3 + security portfolio," rework it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum for all text (body >= 4.5:1, large text >= 3:1); target AAA for primary copy. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support: every reveal and micro-interaction degrades to instant/crossfade. Keyboard-navigable, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, and meaningful alt text. The single amber accent is never the sole carrier of meaning (paired with text/weight).
